Moderation

Being a moderator on Raid Simulator is a unique learning experience, designed to teach the basics of server administration. Most other servers start you off as a chat mod with almost no perms to get the job done. Your aim as a moderator is to get to a stage where all your ban requests are correct and you are confident at handling tickets.

Gags, mutes, and kicks

Gags and Mutes are temporary and don't last from session to session. If a player requires a permanent mute, make a perm ban request.

  • Gags - can be used for players who don’t listen to a warning and are either being overly toxic in voice or are playing ear-rapey music. A couple of other situations may arise although regarding gag lengths, it’s up to you.
  • Mutes - can be used for spam, advertisement or toxicity. If you see something racist or homophobic and you are a mod, mute them then make a ban request. Mute lengths are up to you however I would keep them relatively short and treat them as more of a pulling the players in line sort of thing. Mutes over a couple hours are unnecessary as players won’t usually play for that long.
  • Kicks - are only used as a warning to stop blocking parkour or if a player is committing a bannable offence (This is only allowed via the BATTLEMETRICS kick function) and you need to make them stop while either banning or making a ban request. Make sure you add, “don’t block parkour” if you kick for that reason.

As of 09/01/2024 Moderators can now ban for 1 DAY. This has been implemented to allow Moderators to handle racism bans and give Admins a little bit of time to update/go through the ban requests in discord.

In order for Moderators to be able to ban they MUST be trained on it. See Below

How to use Battlemetrics "BM"

Battlemetrics is or will be your best friend. Other than commands issued in-game Battlemetrics is the MAIN system rust servers use to track players, issue bans, and watch over players in-game.
In Battlemetrics you can and will, issue bans, kick players, make notes, watch chat, and investigate / track players


Welcome to Battlemetrics!, above you will see the general overall and landing page for Battlemetrics
  • On the left and right side, you will see our two servers, NA and EU Raid Simulator
  • Each server shows a list of players' names, next to their names is a small drop-down arrow
  • Within the dropdown menu See below, you can kick, mute, and ban a player (You will mostly be using kick/mute from this menu)

  • You can also click on the player's name in blue, and thus will appear their "Battlemetrics Profile"
  • This is one of the most used terms for staff members
  • Within a player's Battlemetrics profile you will see their current/past bans, their join/leave log, their chat log, their kill log, and any alts that have been associated with their IP address
  • The link to a player's battlemetrics profile is the web address when you are on this webpage, sending other staff members the link will allow us to see the battlemetrics profile you are looking at aswell
Examples below


  • There are a couple of special flags/warnings you can notice on a players account, one is:
    This means a player has an alt account that has been detected under their IP address. You can click the dropdown arrow next to this flag to view the associated account. If a player has 20 accounts, it's most likely a VPN, and those accounts aren't theirs. Or... it will tell you
    If you click the dropdown and the player only has 2-4 accounts under it, it could very much be real. Clicking another blue name will show you the other account under their IP address. At that point, you can check the history of that alt account and any bans/punishments/game bans on that account.

    If you find an alt account that has a game ban under 150 days you MUST escalate to Management to review the alt account. IP addresses are scrambled and you are not allowed to see them, management are the only ones who can confirm the IP address is NOT a VPN.

  • The other flag you will see is:

    This means a player has a game ban on their account... THIS FLAG IS WRONG. A couple of years ago rust closed their public database that used to verify/show a player has a Rust game ban, since then this flag has broken and sometimes says a player's alt account is game banned but they aren't anymore. It also cannot verify their game ban is from rust. The only way for us to know is for you to manually go to their steam account profile page, and see if the game ban was under 150 days old. If it is, issue a ban and FWD to Admin+ to double-check your work


    Issuing a ban

  1. On the players battlemetrics profile, click the big red "Ban Player" button in the top right
  2. Next, you'll see a list of our servers "NA or EU", select the appropriate one
  3. Then, select your "Preset Time", You will only have access to 1 day so select that.
  4. You will now have a dropdown list of preset reasons for why a player was banned. Select the appropriate one
  5. Fill in your notes and input your required evidence
  6. Finally, click save ban at the bottom

Congrats! You have successfully banned a player!
